# Operation PAWS — Donations (Anti‑Scam, Ring‑Fenced Funding)

**Last updated:** 23 April 2026

This page defines how Operation PAWS handles donations ethically and transparently.

## 1) Anti-scam rule (non-negotiable)
Operation PAWS will never request donations via:
- random personal wallet addresses sent through DMs,
- personal bank accounts of individuals,
- “urgent” payment demands via WhatsApp forwards.

Only trust donation methods linked from the official QR hub page (`/docs/start/`) and this file.

## 2) Funding principles
- Ring-fenced: funds should be separated from personal finances.
- Auditable: every funded item should have proof-of-payment and a basic allocation record.
- Welfare-first: money is used to support screening, welfare-safe logistics, and verified programme costs.

## 3) Donation methods (phased)
### Phase 0 (now)
- No donation collection until a proper channel is confirmed.
- Sponsors can support by sponsoring infrastructure (domain, printing, hosting) directly via agreed invoicing.

### Phase 1 (recommended)
Use a credible fundraising platform / partner channel that can:
- accept international payments,
- provide clean statements/records,
- separate programme funds from individuals.

(Add links here once chosen.)

## 4) Crypto (optional, later — only with controls)
Crypto donations are powerful but high-risk without governance.

If/when enabled:
- use a compliant, auditable intake method (prefer “crypto in → convert to fiat → ring‑fenced bank”),
- publish a crypto donation policy (accepted assets, liquidation rules, screening, reporting),
- publish reconciled monthly summaries (aggregate only).

Until then: no crypto wallet addresses are published.

## 5) What donors receive (recommended)
- confirmation message/receipt from the donation platform/partner,
- monthly non-sensitive programme summary (counts + outcomes),
- optional sponsor acknowledgement (opt-in).

## 6) What donations fund (examples)
- welfare screening days (SPCA fees if applicable),
- microchip scanning logistics and admin,
- safe transport (fuel, crates) where approved,
- documentation, printing, and verification systems.

## 7) Transparency
Operation PAWS aims to publish:
- aggregate counts (leads, prescreens, assessed, accepted, not selected),
- anonymised PAWS references and stages,
without exposing personal data or operationally sensitive details.
