# Candidate Selection Criteria (Try-outs)

Not every dog is a working dog. To save time for donors, SPCA, and SAPS, we use the following strict criteria for the "Carletonville 10" pilot and beyond.

## 1. Priority Breeds
- **Patrol/Dual Purpose:** German Shepherd (Working lines), Belgian Malinois, Dutch Shepherd.
- **Detection (Narcotics/Explosives/S&R):** Labrador Retriever, English Springer Spaniel, Cocker Spaniel, Bloodhound.
- **Note:** Healthy cross-breeds of the above will be considered on a case-by-case basis.

## 2. Age & Health
- **Ideal Age:** 12 months to 24 months. (Under 12 months are considered "Pups-in-waiting").
- **Health:** 
    - No history of hip/elbow dysplasia.
    - Full vaccination record (Up to date).
    - Sterilization is NOT required initially (SAPS often prefers intact dogs for specific roles, but this is subject to final SAPS policy).
    - Clear of any chronic skin or heart conditions.

## 3. Temperament & Drive (The "Must-Haves")
We look for the **"Four Pillars of Drive"**:
- **Toy Drive:** Is the dog obsessed with a ball or tug? Will they search for it if hidden?
- **Food Drive:** Is the dog highly motivated by treats? (Essential for reward-based training).
- **Courage:** Does the dog approach new objects/sounds with curiosity rather than fear?
- **Sociability:** Is the dog neutral or friendly to humans? (Aggressive dogs are disqualified from the PAWS pipeline).

## 4. Disqualifying "Red Flags"
- Fear of loud noises or new environments (Environmental shyness).
- Human aggression or extreme dog-to-dog aggression.
- Lack of interest in play/toys (Low drive).
- Physical unsoundness or poor conformation.
