# SPCA Partner Journey: Welfare First

This guide explains how Operation PAWS works from the perspective of an SPCA Inspector or Welfare Officer.

## 1. Pre-Screening (Digital)
- **Action:** PAWS Coordinator shares a "Pre-qualified" list of dogs for a screening day.
- **Benefit:** SPCA is only involved with dogs that already meet basic breed and age criteria, saving inspector time.

## 2. Screening Day (The "Combine")
- **The SPCA Station:** Every dog's first stop is the SPCA booth.
- **Step 1: Microchip Scan:** Confirm identity and link to the PAWS Ref.
- **Step 2: Basic Welfare Check:** Teeth, skin, weight, and handling tolerance.
- **Step 3: The "Welfare Veto":** If an inspector sees a red flag (e.g., untreated injury), they mark the dog as "Not Recommended" on welfare grounds. This is a final decision.

## 3. Post-Screening Outcomes
- **Successful Dogs:** Move to SAPS assessment or Academy.
- **Unsuccessful Dogs:** If owner-surrendered, they move into the SPCA's standard rehoming pipeline.
- **Traceability:** SPCA can verify any dog's status at any time via the [Public Tracker](../../docs/tracker/).
