# SAPS K9 Journey: Capability & Acceptance

This guide explains how Operation PAWS simplifies the intake of donated dogs for SAPS K9 Unit evaluators.

## 1. Receiving Pre-Screened Candidates
- **The "PAWS Pack":** SAPS receives a candidate with a full history: PAWS Ref, breed, age, vaccination proof, and a preliminary toy-drive score.
- **Benefit:** SAPS does not waste time assessing dogs that are unhealthy or have low drive.

## 2. Official Assessment
- **Role:** SAPS Evaluators conduct their own standard operational tests.
- **Support:** PAWS Coaches assist with handling and logistics so SAPS can focus on evaluation.

## 3. The Handover (SAPS 505a)
- **Step 1: Verification:** SAPS confirms the microchip matches the PAWS record.
- **Step 2: Documentation:** SAPS signs the official **SAPS 505(a)** and **SAPS 84**.
- **Step 3: Transfer:** The dog officially becomes a state asset.

## 4. Anti-Corruption / Smuggling Defense
- Every dog SAPS accepts is publicly marked as "SAPS Accepted" on the tracker. 
- This transparency protects the Unit from false allegations of unauthorized dog sales or smuggling.
