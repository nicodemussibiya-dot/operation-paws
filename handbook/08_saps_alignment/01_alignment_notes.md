# SAPS Policy Alignment & FAQ

This document prepares the Operation PAWS team for meetings with SAPS provincial and national representatives by aligning project terminology with official SAPS instructions.

## 1. Do SAPS dogs have "Employee Badges"?
**Reality:** No. 
SAPS dogs are classified as service animals and state assets. They are tracked via:
- **Microchip:** The non-negotiable primary identification.
- **SAPS 84 (History File):** A formal file that travels with the dog.
- **PAS (Asset Register):** Animals are registered on the PAS system with a specific serial number.
- **Donation Register:** Records the donation against a fair value.

**PAWS Strategy:** We use the term "PAWS Reference Number" as a public-facing tracking key that links to these internal SAPS identifiers.

## 2. Official Donation Form: SAPS 505(a)
**Reference:** SAPS National Instruction 6 of 2018.
- The official form is **SAPS 505(a)**: *"Deed of unconditional and irrevocable donation inter vivos"*.
- **Action:** In meetings, request the latest SAPS 505(a) template. 
- **Rule:** Even if a dog is found unsuitable after assessment, a record of the SAPS 505(a) process is typically kept for auditing purposes.

## 3. Custody & Premises Rules
- **Private Animals:** SAPS policy is strict: privately owned animals may not be housed with official animals on state premises.
- **Assessment Window:** If SAPS keeps a private dog for assessment, it must be kept separate and typically for a maximum of 30 days at designated units.
- **PAWS Strategy:** Tier 3 (Academy) dogs remain privately owned and are trained **off SAPS premises** (e.g., at SPCA or partner sites) until they are ready for the official SAPS 505(a) step.

## 4. Addressing "Smuggling" Allegations
To counter any narrative of "smuggling for profit":
- **Microchip Rule:** No dog moves without a scanned and logged microchip.
- **Chain of Custody:** Every handover (Owner -> Academy -> SAPS) is documented with a timestamped microchip scan.
- **Public Tracker:** Our aggregate public tracker proves the volumes and outcomes honestly, showing that non-accepted dogs return to donors or SPCA rehoming.
